Short-term rental fit.

Best for larger groups

3 Bedroom / 2 Bath Townhome

Beds
3
Baths
2
Type
Townhome

Pros

  • Highest revenue upside from larger groups and stronger weekend premiums.
  • Wider guest segments: families, friend groups, events, and multi-gen travel.
  • Can stand out as a sleeps 6-8 option when photos and amenities are strong.
  • Longer stays are more likely, which can reduce turnover frequency.

Cons

  • Highest operational complexity: more beds, linens, supplies, and readiness checks.
  • More guests can mean more wear, damage risk, noise concerns, and utility load.
  • Cleaning cost and quality control are harder, which can affect reviews.
  • Superhost risk is higher because expectations are bigger and misses are more visible.

Best for

Family markets, drive-to destinations, events, theme parks, ski towns, and beach towns.

Balanced STR option

2 Bedroom / 2 Bath Apartment

Beds
2
Baths
2
Type
Apartment

Pros

  • Best Superhost balance: strong demand with manageable operations.
  • Two bathrooms improve satisfaction for couples, families, and business travelers.
  • Flexible for two couples, family stays, work partners, nurses, and relocations.
  • Easier to keep 5-star clean than a 3/2, with better upside than a 1/1.

Cons

  • More competition because 2/2 layouts are a common short-term rental sweet spot.
  • Must win with photos, design, amenities, guest experience, and reviews.
  • Still has moderate turnover work with more linens and setup than a 1/1.
  • Pricing discipline matters so the calendar fills without underpricing revenue.

Best for

Most markets. Usually the strongest layout when optimizing for Superhost and revenue.

Lowest setup complexity

1 Bedroom / 1 Bath Apartment

Beds
1
Baths
1
Type
Apartment

Pros

  • Easiest to operate with faster cleaning, simpler inventory, and fewer issues.
  • Lower upfront furnishing and maintenance costs.
  • Great for consistency and repeatable 5-star experiences.
  • Can perform well midweek with business travelers, couples, and short stays.

Cons

  • Lower revenue ceiling because nightly rate and occupancy are capped.
  • More sensitive to seasonality and supply in high-competition markets.
  • Misses higher-value family and group weekend demand.
  • Noise or neighbor sensitivity can still matter in dense buildings or HOAs.

Best for

Urban and business markets, hospitals, universities, downtown areas, and couples getaways.
